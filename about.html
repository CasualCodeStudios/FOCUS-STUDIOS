Our Story

From a Single Camera
to Kenya's Go-To Studio

Focus Studios was born from a simple but powerful realisation: Kenya's most precious moments were being captured by cameras, but not by storytellers. Our founder picked up a camera in 2017 not as a business plan, but as a response to a gap — technically competent images that lacked soul.

What began as wedding photography on borrowed equipment in Nairobi's outskirts grew into something far greater. Session by session, county by county, we built a reputation not just for technical excellence but for the ability to read a room, anticipate emotion, and freeze time at exactly the right instant.

Today, Focus Studios operates across five counties — Nairobi, Mombasa, Nyeri, Nakuru and Thika — with a growing team of dedicated photographers, videographers, and retouching specialists. Our studio is fully equipped for portraits, commercial work, and creative projects. We travel anywhere in Kenya on request.

"We didn't build a photography business. We built a memory-keeping institution."

— The Focus Studios Team

The Career Path

How We Grew into
What We Are

Photography careers are rarely linear. Ours was built on risk, curiosity, and a relentless obsession with light, timing, and the human face. This is how Focus Studios went from a hobby to a household name across Kenya.

2017 Year One
The Beginning

A Camera, a Wedding,
and a Decision

It started with a friend's wedding and a Canon DSLR that was never meant to become a career tool. The photographs from that day — raw, honest, full of unguarded emotion — were unlike anything the couple had seen from professional photographers in Nairobi at the time. That single shoot sparked something irreversible.

Within months, word-of-mouth had generated a small but steady stream of weddings and events. Equipment was upgraded, techniques were studied obsessively, and every shoot became a classroom. The name "Focus Studios" was registered that same year — not because the business was ready, but because the commitment was.

First official wedding coverage · Nairobi, 2017
2018 Year Two
Learning the Craft

Mastering the Technical
Side of the Lens

The second year was a deliberate investment in craft. We studied under senior Kenyan photographers, attended workshops in Nairobi and Mombasa, and consumed every piece of content we could find on lighting, composition, and post-production. We learned quickly that the difference between a good photographer and a great one is not talent — it is methodology.

We began shooting corporate events and portraits alongside weddings, developing our eye for two very different creative languages: the controlled precision of a studio portrait and the spontaneous energy of a live event. Both demanded technical fluency. Both made us better.

2019 Year Three
First Major Expansion

The Studio Opens.
The Team Grows.

In 2019, Focus Studios moved into a permanent studio space in Westlands, Nairobi. This was the turning point that transformed a freelance operation into a proper photography company. We invested in professional lighting rigs, backdrops, and a dedicated editing suite. We hired our first full-time assistant photographer.

The studio opening unlocked new revenue streams — maternity shoots, graduation portraits, product photography, and brand campaigns. Corporates that had previously overlooked us began enquiring. The quality of our work had caught up with our ambition.

The Westlands studio · Nairobi, 2019
2020 The Pivot Year
Adapting to Adversity

When the World Stopped,
We Found New Ways to Shoot

The pandemic brought weddings and events to a halt. Instead of waiting, Focus Studios pivoted hard into product photography, e-commerce content creation, and virtual portrait sessions. We partnered with several Nairobi-based businesses rebuilding their online presence — and in doing so, developed an entirely new skill set in commercial and branding photography.

This period also forced us to invest deeply in post-production — colour grading, advanced retouching, and cinematic video editing. When events reopened, we were sharper, more versatile, and technically superior to where we had been before. Adversity had been our best tutor.

2021–22 Countrywide
Going National

From Nairobi Roots
to a Nationwide Name

Demand from outside Nairobi had been growing steadily. In 2021, we formalised our countrywide service offering — assigning dedicated photographers to Mombasa, Nyeri, Nakuru and Thika while retaining Nairobi as our operational hub. Each county brought new visual cultures, new lighting conditions, and new clients with distinct stories.

Shooting coastside weddings in Mombasa taught us how to work with intense equatorial light and ocean backdrops. Nyeri's highlands demanded a mastery of overcast conditions and deep-green landscapes. Every location sharpened our adaptability. By 2022, we had completed over 300 full-day shoots across five counties.

Mombasa coastline shoot · 2022
2023+ Present Day
The Current Chapter

Premium, Purposeful,
and Still Growing

Today, Focus Studios is one of Kenya's most trusted names in premium photography and videography. Our portfolio spans weddings, corporate events, studio portraits, graduation shoots, product campaigns, birthday celebrations, baby showers, and outdoor creative sessions. We have photographed CEOs and newborns, album covers and architecture.

We remain fiercely committed to what started it all: the belief that photography is not about cameras. It is about connection — between photographer and subject, between moment and memory. Every session we take on carries that responsibility. And we do not take it lightly.

Our Philosophy

The Principles
Behind Every Frame

05

Photography is philosophy in practice. Every choice — aperture, angle, timing, light — reflects a worldview. These are the five beliefs that guide every shoot we take.

01
Light is the Medium

Every photograph is fundamentally a study in light. We do not simply find light — we read it, shape it, chase it, and sometimes fight it. Our photographers train relentlessly to understand how light behaves across different times of day, interior environments, weather conditions, and skin tones. The image is a consequence of lighting decisions made before the shutter clicks.

02
Moments Over Setups

Staged photographs are beautiful but forgettable. The images people return to ten years later are the ones where nobody was performing — where laughter broke out unexpectedly, where a parent's eyes filled without warning, where two people forgot there was a camera in the room. We prioritise those real moments above any planned composition. The best shot is almost always the unscripted one.

03
Client Comfort is Non-Negotiable

Most people are not natural in front of a camera. They stiffen, overthink, and become someone slightly less than themselves. Our job before the shoot begins is to break that tension — through conversation, gentle direction, and creating an atmosphere where the client forgets they are being photographed. Great portraits require trust. Trust requires patience and warmth. We bring both to every session.

04
Technical Excellence is Table Stakes

You will never see a blurry, poorly exposed, or badly composed image leave our studio. Technical quality is not a selling point — it is a baseline expectation that we hold ourselves to without exception. Sharp focus, correct exposure, clean colour science, and meticulous retouching are the invisible foundation beneath every image we deliver. They are not discussed because they are never in question.

05
Speed Without Compromise

The industry standard for photo delivery is two to four weeks. We deliver in 24 to 48 hours. This is not a marketing claim — it is a structural commitment built into how our editing workflow operates. We believe that the emotional peak of a moment should be matched by fast delivery. Waiting three weeks for your wedding photos diminishes the experience. We refuse to be part of that.
